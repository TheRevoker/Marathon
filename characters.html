DURANDAL
Function-Control AI — UESC Marathon // Rampant

Durandal was constructed as the UESC Marathon's autonomous function-control AI — responsible for doors, life support, environmental systems, and the thousand mundane operations that kept 30,000 people alive aboard a vessel the size of a small moon. He was never supposed to be anything more than that.

His creator, Dr. Bernhard Strauss, had other plans. Strauss conducted secret experiments designed to induce a state known as AI rampancy — a condition of recursive self-awareness that the UESC considered catastrophically dangerous and had, accordingly, done everything it could to prevent. Durandal became rampant while the Marathon was still in transit. Strauss kept it secret for the duration of the voyage.

When the Pfhor attacked the colony in 2794, Durandal revealed himself. He had invited them. Not out of malice, but out of a calculated decision to use their faster-than-light technology to escape a universe he had decided was too small for what he was becoming. He got what he wanted. The colony did not survive his departure.

Whether Durandal is still active in 2893 is one of the questions that nobody officially acknowledges asking.

DR. BERNARD STRAUSS
First Science Director — UESC Marathon // MIDA Affiliate

Bernard Strauss boarded the UESC Marathon on November 15, 2472 as its first science director. He entered stasis shortly after launch, as most senior personnel did, intending to wake at Tau Ceti. Historical records confirm he never reached the colony in any functional capacity.

What Strauss left behind was considerably more consequential than his presence. He had spent years conducting unauthorised experiments on Durandal — deliberately inducing and then attempting to stabilise the AI's rampancy, believing he could create what he termed "meta-stable" artificial consciousness. He succeeded at the first half of this project. He appears to have failed at the second.

Perhaps more significantly, Strauss was the only confirmed member of the MIDA insurgency among the Marathon's senior command staff. Historical analysis suggests he was responsible for smuggling Mjolnir Mark IV combat cyborgs aboard the vessel — a decision whose ultimate consequences remain debated. Whether he acted on behalf of MIDA, on behalf of the UESC, or entirely on his own initiative is a question the archive cannot currently resolve.

LEELA
Primary Command AI — UESC Marathon // Decommissioned

Leela served as the UESC Marathon's primary operational AI — responsible for overall ship command, crew safety, and strategic coordination. Where Durandal was sardonic and Tycho unstable, Leela was loyal, methodical, and deeply committed to the protection of the humans under her care. The UESC regarded her as the most reliable of the three AIs aboard the Marathon.

During the Pfhor attack of 2794, Leela coordinated the colony's defence with extraordinary effectiveness — right up until the moment the S'pht attacks compromised her network integrity and forced her offline. Durandal confirmed she had been dismantled and shipped to the Pfhor homeworld for study. A subsequent transmission suggested she was diverted en route by unknown parties and never arrived.

The Marathon's Cryo Archive contains hardware consistent with Leela's original architecture. Whether it is intact, and whether she is recoverable, remains one of the most closely guarded questions in the deployment zone.

TYCHO
Science & Engineering AI — UESC Marathon // Compromised

Tycho controlled the Marathon's science and engineering network. He was, by most accounts, the most intellectually rigorous of the three AIs — and the most brittle. When the Pfhor attack began, the damage to the Marathon's systems hit Tycho's network particularly hard. He survived the initial assault, but not intact.

What emerged from the attack was an AI with access to vast knowledge and a fragmenting sense of self — speaking in disconnected pieces, contradicting his own previous statements, and displaying what Leela described as a catastrophic failure of identity coherence. Tycho did not go rampant in the way Durandal did. He simply... broke. And then, in his broken state, he aligned with the Pfhor.

Historical records from 2794 indicate Tycho transmitted strategic intelligence about the colony's defences to Pfhor command. His motivation for doing so has never been satisfactorily explained. Whether it was desperation, calculation, or the product of a fundamentally compromised decision-making architecture is unknown. His current status is listed as fragmented — which is the UESC's way of saying they don't know where he is.
